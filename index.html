Navigate The Singing Rivers

What will be regarded as human history is currently mediated by digital algorithms.

This mediation is plagued with colonial remnants.

These algorithms become tools for epistemicide when filtering out voices from the informational mainstream.

As smaller tributaries of a wider river, non-western collective memories tend

to flow on arid sides, slowly evaporating while the main body of liquid information gets mightier.

In the case of Artificial Intelligence, the situation gets complicated:

as Machine Learning models learn from patterns, it is not only relevant to think about the method itself,

but the data that one feeds the system with.

As our existence becomes inevitably quantified, the presence of this "other" data is rebellious:

by reclaiming these affluent streams of information, these data-sets are recolonized

hoping to override yet certainly transforming the main-stream, fluent again in

its natural polyphony as the Singing Rivers of a complex human story.
